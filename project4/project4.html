Principal Component Analysis (PCA) is a way to visualize highly dimentional data on a 2d plain. In this case
we try to show the outline of a hand as a single point in a scatter plot.
When you mouse over the points in the pca plot the right graph will load the corresponding hand outline. When
you mouse over different points you'll see that points closer together correspond to hand that look more a like.
When you look around a bit you'll see that points close to 0,0 represent a normal hand outline. Going along the
Y-axis you'll see that on top, the upper most fingers will stretch away from eachother. Likewise, going down on
the y-axis you'll see that the bottem few fingers will stretch away from eachother. The x-axis defines how
much the fingers are stretches out in general.
